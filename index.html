Problem Statement: Money Management

College students need a better way to manage their money.

Affinity diagram: Money Management in College

This affinity diagram represents 5 categories to help college students manage their money while in school. These categories include advertising, essential features, hurdles, product ideas, costs and expenses. Advertising goes into detail about how we will let students know about this resource available to them. Essential features/product ideas are some programs that will help students improve their financial skills. Hurdles are some challenges that can potentially happen during start up. Costs and expenses is about making everything we offer affordable.

Team Personas: Mary Brown

Mary Brown is a typical college student, struggles to manage her finances but aspires to find a balance between enjoying her college life, paying bills, and planning for her future. She believes that Managing money is a journey.

Team Storyboards: Mary Simplifies Money Management

Mary is overwhelemed with bills when she decides to download our money management app to help her track expenses, make a budget, and set reminders for when bills are due. This releases a lot of stress off of Mary to help her focus more on her academics
